Family Life

When my parents were in their early twenties, they decided to try to escape the communist Slovak regime and immigrate to America. After spending over a year at an Austrian refugee camp, they finally became eligable and landed in Houston, Texas. There, they each got degrees from the Univeristy of Houston. A year after I was born in 1999, my parents moved to California where my father started his own private practice as an attourney, and my mother worked for an accounting firm. Today, my father still has his practice and my mother teaches yoga at a local studio.

I was raised in a home where homework had to be finished before playing outside, family dinners were a mandatory, nightly routine, and electronics had to be off by 11pm. As a family, we spent alot of time together -- whether it was planning a family outing, going out to dinner, or watching a movie together before bed. My siblings and I were given alot of freedom to explore our own interests academically and socially, but were also under a strict set of rules that bared consequences if we stepped out of line. My parents cared for us deeply and did everything they could to provide opportunities for us. They taught us to speak Slovak to be able to connect to our native culture, made us play sports to keep us phycsially active and stay out of trouble, and took us around the world for us us to gain perspective and humility.
